RIT Alumni Foundation:

The RIT Alumni Foundation is an entity created by alumni of Lambda Phi Epsilon, Beta Epsilon chapter of RIT. First and foremost, it was created for alumni and is run by alumni. We formed a partnership with the Lambda Phi Epsilon Foundation so that tax-exempt charitable donations can be made to an endowment fund set up for us.

In order to keep the RIT Alumni Foundation running, we pay an annual $500 maintenance fee, so we must make the best use of it.

The purpose of the RIT Alumni Foundation is to bestow merit and need-based scholarships, along with other financial and social incentives.

We intend to award an annual $500 scholarship to the "RIT Lambda of the Year". The scholarship amount is subject to change in the future. Funds can also be used for social events, such as brotherhood retreats and anniversaries.

This is one of the best ways to pay less in taxes AND save up funds for the future. This money will eventually go back to you and our chapter. With a company match, your donation is automatically doubled. Wouldn't you like to secure your spot at an event with a premium venue? üôÇ

The Lambda Phi Epsilon Foundation is a 501(c)(3) non-profit organization (EIN: 81-0748181). The purpose of the Lambda Phi Epsilon Foundation is to engage in charitable activities and provide educational support to members of Lambda Phi Epsilon International Fraternity, Incorporated.

The Board:

The purpose of the board is to vote upon where funds go to and how much. The board was structured to have at least 1 alumnus from each class to maintain a diverse opinion. It is necessary to have input from brothers of all ages, especially as the chapter gets older.

Currently, the members sitting on the board are Christopher Savan, Hess Lee, Mitchel Zaarib, Jacky Ren, Wilson Huang, and Saad Khan. Members of the board are subject to change in the future. We are still in the process of forming guidelines and protocols that dictate how the board runs.

If you are interested in joining the board, please let Christopher Savan know.

Donations:

There are other ways to donate. However, PayPal is the preferred payments system because it's currently set up in a way where after every donation, it triggers an event that runs automation for bookkeeping, emails, Slack notifications, storing information in a database, and sending tax receipts.

If you really have no other choice, you may Venmo @lfefoundation.

The funds are stored in an endowment fund account through Fidelity.
The funds will be used to pay the annual maintenance fee, fund the annual scholarship, and fund brotherhood events.
Tax Implications:

A tax deduction is an amount that you can deduct from your taxable income to lower the amount of taxes that you owe. This applies to all donations to the RIT Alumni Foundation.

Read more about deductibility code and exemption type for the Lambda Phi Epsilon Foundation on irs.gov.

Please consult with your company's HR or benefits coordinator. They will be able to walk you through making charitable donations with a company match.

For example, for GoDaddy, I can make charitable donations through a third-party portal. Another way I can do it is to first make a donation through PayPal and wait until I get the tax receipt from the Lambda Phi Epsilon Foundation. Once I have the tax receipt, I can send a request to GoDaddy stating that I made a donation to a 501(c)(3) organization and attach the tax receipt. GoDaddy will match up to $1500 every fiscal year, but that does not mean I will donate $1500.

Check if your employer sponsors matching gift programs here.

The Lambda Phi Epsilon Foundation will forward tax receipts to the email that you use for your PayPal or Venmo account. Make sure to keep the tax receipts. You may need to provide this to your company to get the match. It may also come in handy when filing your taxes.
